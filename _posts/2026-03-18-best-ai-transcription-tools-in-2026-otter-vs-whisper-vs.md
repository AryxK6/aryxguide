<p>AI transcription has reached the point where human-level accuracy is achievable on clear audio, at a fraction of what professional transcription used to cost. The tools have different strengths depending on your use case. Here's where they stand.</p>

<figure><img alt="AI transcription tools comparison 2026" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1478737270239-2f02b77fc618?w=1200&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>

<h2>Otter.ai Best for Meetings</h2>
<p>Otter integrates directly with Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams. It joins meetings automatically, transcribes in real time, identifies speakers, and generates a summary with action items when the meeting ends. For anyone who attends a lot of meetings and needs notes without manually taking them, this is genuinely useful.</p>
<p>The free plan gives 300 transcription minutes per month and 3 AI summaries. Paid starts at $10/month. The accuracy on standard business meetings with clear audio and native English speakers is around 90–95%.</p>

<h2>OpenAI Whisper Best Free Option for Files</h2>
<figure><img alt="Audio file transcription using AI tools" src="https://i.supaimg.com/e2d7b027-0330-470f-ab98-2277696b7201/7079fdf5-8408-4d77-af78-a7a7fd898106.png?w=900&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>
<p>Whisper is OpenAI's open-source transcription model. It's free to use if you run it locally (requires Python and some technical setup) and available through various web interfaces for free or low cost. Accuracy is excellent comparable to paid tools for most audio quality.</p>
<p>The limitation: no live transcription, no meeting integration, no automatic summaries. It's a transcription engine, not a workflow tool. If you have audio files that need converting to text and you're comfortable with basic technical setup, Whisper is hard to beat on cost.</p>

<h2>Descript Best When Transcription Is Part of Editing</h2>
<p>If you're editing podcast audio or video and need transcription as part of that process, Descript is the obvious choice because it combines both. Transcription quality is on par with Otter. Where Descript stands out is the post-transcription workflow: the transcript becomes the editing interface, so you're not just getting text you're getting an editable document that controls the underlying audio.</p>

<h2>Rev For Accuracy-Critical Professional Use</h2>
<p>Rev offers both AI transcription (cheaper, 85–90% accuracy) and human transcription (more expensive, 99%+ accuracy). For legal, medical, or academic use where errors have consequences, human transcription is still the right choice. Rev's human transcripts cost around $1.50 per minute and typically turn around within a few hours.</p>

<h2>Which to Use</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Frequent meeting notes: Otter.ai</li>
  <li>Podcast/video editing: Descript</li>
  <li>Audio files, free option: Whisper</li>
  <li>Legal or professional transcription needing high accuracy: Rev human</li>
</ul>
