<p>The most underused content strategy for small creators is repurposing. You spend hours writing a 1,500-word blog post and publish it once. With AI tools, that same post can feed a week's worth of content across multiple platforms. Here's the exact workflow.</p>

<figure><img alt="Content repurposing strategy across social platforms" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1611926653458-09294b3142bf?w=1200&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>

<h2>Start With a Strong Source Post</h2>
<p>Repurposing only works when the source material is good. A thin blog post produces thin repurposed content. Before running a post through any AI tool, make sure it has: a clear thesis, specific examples or data, and at least 3–4 distinct points worth extracting.</p>

<h2>The 10-Piece Breakdown</h2>
<figure><img alt="Social media content calendar and planning" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1432888498266-38ffec3eaf0a?w=900&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>
<ol>
  <li><strong>LinkedIn post.</strong> Paste your blog post into ChatGPT and ask: "Write a LinkedIn post based on the main argument of this article. Start with a hook. No hashtag spam. 200–300 words."</li>
  <li><strong>Twitter/X thread.</strong> Ask for the top 5–7 points as individual tweets, each under 280 characters. The intro tweet should make someone want to read the thread.</li>
  <li><strong>Short-form video script.</strong> Ask for a 60-second script covering the single most useful insight from the post. Record it as a Reel or TikTok.</li>
  <li><strong>Email newsletter section.</strong> Ask for a 150-word summary written as if you're telling a friend about something useful you learned. Link to the full post.</li>
  <li><strong>Pinterest pin descriptions.</strong> Ask for 3 pin descriptions (150 characters each) targeting different keyword angles from the post.</li>
  <li><strong>FAQ section.</strong> Ask AI to generate 5 questions readers might have after reading the post. Add these to the bottom of the original article for SEO.</li>
  <li><strong>Quote graphics.</strong> Ask for 3 quotable sentences from the post. Drop each into Canva as a quote graphic for Instagram or LinkedIn.</li>
  <li><strong>Podcast talking points.</strong> Ask for an outline of 4–5 discussion points you could cover in a 10-minute podcast episode on the same topic.</li>
  <li><strong>Related post ideas.</strong> Ask for 5 related blog post topics that build on what this post covers. Use these for your next content calendar.</li>
  <li><strong>Updated SEO title variations.</strong> Ask for 10 alternative title options targeting different keyword angles. Test one of these as an A/B experiment.</li>
</ol>

<h2>How Long This Actually Takes</h2>
<p>With a good source post and ChatGPT open, the full workflow takes about 30–45 minutes. You're not accepting the outputs as-is you're editing each one to add your voice. But the generation step cuts the work in half compared to writing each piece from scratch.</p>
<p>One well-researched post per week + this repurposing workflow = consistent presence across 4–5 platforms without burning out. That's the actual value.</p>
