<p>Video editing used to require either expensive software expertise or expensive editors. Neither is true anymore. AI video tools have gotten good enough that a creator with zero editing background can produce a polished video in under an hour. Here's what's actually worth using.</p>

<figure><img alt="AI video editing software on computer screen 2026" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1574717024653-61fd2cf4d44d?w=1200&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>

<h2>Descript The Tool That Changed Talking-Head Editing</h2>
<p>Descript lets you edit video by editing a transcript. Delete a sentence from the text document and the corresponding video clip is removed automatically. For interview footage, course content, or any talking-head video, this is the most intuitive editing workflow available.</p>
<p>The filler word removal feature one click to delete every "um," "uh," and long pause alone saves 20–30 minutes on a typical 10-minute video. Descript also generates captions automatically, which are accurate enough to require only minor corrections.</p>
<p>Pricing: free plan with limited exports, paid from $24/month. For anyone creating regular video content, this is the easiest upgrade to justify.</p>

<h2>CapCut Best Free Option for Short-Form</h2>
<figure><img alt="Short form video creation on mobile phone" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1611162616305-c69b3fa7fbe0?w=1200&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>
<p>CapCut is free and genuinely good for Reels, TikToks, and YouTube Shorts. The AI features include auto-captions, background removal, voice enhancement, and a script-to-video tool that generates a rough cut from text. The mobile app is more capable than most desktop editors charged as a subscription.</p>
<p>The limitation: it's optimized for short-form. Editing a 30-minute tutorial in CapCut is possible but not pleasant.</p>

<h2>Runway ML For Visual Effects Without a VFX Budget</h2>
<p>Runway's Gen-2 and Gen-3 models can generate video clips from text prompts or extend existing footage. For creators who need B-roll visuals, abstract backgrounds, or stylized clips they can't film themselves, Runway fills a real gap.</p>
<p>It's not cheap ($15/month and up) and the outputs still have an AI look that experienced viewers notice. But for supplemental footage rather than primary content, it works.</p>

<h2>Adobe Premiere Pro with AI Features For Serious Editors</h2>
<p>Adobe has added significant AI features to Premiere Pro: auto-reframe (automatically resizes content for different aspect ratios), text-based editing (similar to Descript), and scene detection. If you're already in the Adobe ecosystem, these features add real value to a tool you're already paying for.</p>

<h2>Which to Start With</h2>
<p>Start with CapCut for free. If you do primarily talking-head content and want to get serious, add Descript. Runway is a specialist tool for specific use cases, not an everyday editor. Premiere is for people who already know they need professional-grade software.</p>
