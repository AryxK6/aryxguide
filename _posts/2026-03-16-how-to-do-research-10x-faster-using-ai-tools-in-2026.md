<p>Research is still necessary. AI hasn't eliminated the need to understand a topic before writing about it if anything, the bar for original, well-researched content has risen because generic content is now so easy to produce. What AI has changed is how fast you can move through the research process. Here's the workflow.</p>

<figure><img alt="AI research tools and process 2026" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1456324504439-367cee3b3c32?w=1200&q=80" style="border-radius: 8px; margin: 16px 0px; width: 100%;" /></figure>

<h2>Step 1: Use AI to Map the Topic</h2>
<p>Before reading a single article, ask ChatGPT or Perplexity: "What are the main subtopics, debates, and open questions in [topic]?" This gives you a map of the territory in two minutes. You'll know which areas are settled (don't need deep research) and which are contested or evolving (do need primary sources).</p>

<h2>Step 2: Use Perplexity for Sourced Overviews</h2>
<figure><img alt="Research process using AI on laptop" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1516321318423-f06f85e504b3?w=900&q=80" style="border-radius: 6px; margin: 14px 0px; width: 100%;" /></figure>
<p>Perplexity gives you summarized answers with citations next to each claim, which makes verification fast. Use it for: finding specific statistics, identifying recent studies or reports, and checking whether a claim you heard is supported by actual sources.</p>
<p>Don't cite Perplexity itself follow the citations to the original sources and verify them. AI can misrepresent what a study found. The citation gets you to the right place; you still need to check what it actually says.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Use NotebookLM for Deep Research on Specific Documents</h2>
<p>Google's NotebookLM lets you upload PDFs and then ask questions about their content. For long reports, academic papers, or documents you'd otherwise spend an hour reading, you can ask specific questions and get direct answers with page references. This turns a 60-page industry report into a 10-minute research session.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Verify Anything That Matters</h2>
<p>AI tools hallucinate. Not constantly, but enough that any statistic, quote, or specific claim you plan to publish needs to be verified against a primary source. The rule: if you can't find it confirmed in the original source, don't publish it as a fact.</p>
<p>This step takes time but protects your credibility. Publishing incorrect statistics doesn't just affect one post it affects how readers view everything else on your site.</p>

<h2>The Time Savings in Practice</h2>
<p>Before AI tools, researching a complex topic for a 1,500-word blog post took 2–3 hours of reading and note-taking. With the workflow above AI for topic mapping, Perplexity for sourced overviews, NotebookLM for specific documents it's closer to 45–60 minutes, with the remaining time spent on verification and writing. That's a real productivity gain, not a marginal one.</p>
