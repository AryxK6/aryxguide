<p>Internal linking is the most underused SEO tactic on most blogs. It costs nothing, requires no outreach, and genuinely moves rankings especially for newer sites that don't have many external backlinks. Here's how to approach it strategically rather than randomly.</p>

<figure><img alt="Internal linking strategy for blog SEO 2026" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1562577309-4932fdd64cd1?w=1200&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>

<h2>Why Internal Links Matter</h2>
<p>When you link from one page to another on your own site, you're doing two things: telling Google which of your pages are related, and passing authority from pages that already have it to pages that need it. A post that gets linked to from 10 other pages on your site will rank better than an identical post that sits in isolation.</p>
<p>For new blogs with minimal external backlinks, internal linking is how you distribute whatever authority you do have across your content.</p>

<h2>The Pillar-Cluster Model</h2>
<figure><img alt="Content cluster and pillar page strategy diagram" src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1504868584819-f8e8b4b6d7e3?w=900&q=80" style="border-radius: 10px; margin: 20px 0px; max-width: 100%;" /></figure>
<p>The most effective internal linking structure for blogs is the pillar-cluster model:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Pillar pages</strong> cover a broad topic comprehensively 2,000+ words, targeting a moderately competitive head keyword.</li>
  <li><strong>Cluster posts</strong> cover subtopics of that main topic in more detail, targeting longer-tail keywords.</li>
  <li><strong>All cluster posts link back to the pillar page.</strong> The pillar page links out to all cluster posts.</li>
</ul>
<p>This signals to Google that your pillar page is an authority on the topic and that your cluster posts are relevant, related content. The ranking effect is real and measurable.</p>

<h2>Practical Rules for Internal Linking</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Use descriptive anchor text not "click here" but the actual topic of the page you're linking to.</li>
  <li>Link from your newer posts to older relevant posts to keep older content in Google's attention.</li>
  <li>Aim for 3–5 internal links per post minimum.</li>
  <li>When you publish a new post, go back to 3–4 older related posts and add a link to the new one.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Using AI to Find Internal Linking Opportunities</h2>
<p>Paste a list of your post titles into ChatGPT and ask: "Which of these posts are topically related to [new post topic]? Which should link to it, and which should it link to?" This takes 2 minutes and surfaces connections you might miss manually. It won't be perfect, but it's a good starting point for each new post you publish.</p>
