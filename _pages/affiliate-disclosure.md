<h1>Affiliate Disclosure</h1>

<p><strong>Last Updated: March 26, 2026</strong></p>

<p>ARYX Guide participates in affiliate marketing programs. This page explains what that means, how it works, and what it means for you as a reader.</p>

<h2>What Affiliate Marketing Means on This Site</h2>

<p>Some links on <a href="https://www.aryxguide.site">www.aryxguide.site</a> are affiliate links. When you click one of these links and make a purchase or sign up for a service, ARYX Guide may earn a commission. The commission comes from the company you purchased from, not from you. The price you pay is the same whether you use an affiliate link or go directly to the site.</p>

<p>This is how a portion of this site's operating costs are covered. Running a site, researching tools, and producing long-form content takes time and resources. Affiliate commissions, alongside display advertising, help make that sustainable without charging readers for access.</p>

<h2>Which Programs We Participate In</h2>

<p>ARYX Guide participates in affiliate programs run by various companies in the technology, software, and digital marketing space. These include but are not limited to SaaS products, web hosting services, AI tools, SEO software, and online course platforms. Specific affiliate relationships may change over time as new programs are added or existing ones are discontinued.</p>

<p>Not every link on this site is an affiliate link. Many links, particularly those to research sources, reference materials, or tools we mention in context without a formal partnership, are standard links with no commission attached.</p>

<h2>How Affiliate Relationships Affect What We Write</h2>

<p>They don't, or at least they shouldn't, and I'm going to explain exactly how I try to make sure of that.</p>

<p>The starting point for any recommendation on this site is whether the tool or service is actually useful. I research and test what I cover. If a product has an affiliate program and I think it's worth recommending, I'll join that program. But the recommendation comes first. I don't look for affiliate programs and then build content around them.</p>

<p>I've written critical coverage of products that advertise on this site. I've declined to recommend tools I've been approached about because I didn't think they were good enough. I've given positive coverage to tools that have no affiliate program at all. The affiliate relationship follows the editorial judgment, not the other way around.</p>

<p>That said, I'm one person with my own perspective and blind spots. If you ever feel that a recommendation doesn't hold up, I'd genuinely want to know. The contact form is there for exactly that kind of feedback.</p>

In addition to this page, affiliate links within articles may be identified with a note indicating that the link is an affiliate link.</p>

<p>This disclosure applies site-wide, including all articles, guides, reviews, and comparison posts published on <a href="https://www.aryxguide.site">www.aryxguide.site</a>.</p>
